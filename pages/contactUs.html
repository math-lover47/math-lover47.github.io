FAQ

We always tell the patient and family honestly what we can achieve with rehabilitation at each stage of recovery and what we cannot achieve. We set realistic goals. Sometimes there is a chance of full recovery and sometimes, unfortunately, there is no chance. But we always set ourselves a goal - to normalize a person's life in any situation, to restore the joy of life, and to help them adapt to a new life, and to improve the quality of life, minimizing the risks of possible complications as much as possible.

Until recently, the concept of rehabilitation did not exist in Kazakhstan and many people after serious illnesses and injuries never returned to normal life. Many people still do not understand what it is and who may need it. Let's dot all the i's. Rehabilitation is a complex restoration of the functions of the organism disturbed as a result of illness or injury. The main goal is to improve the quality of life of a person. In rehabilitation, it is crucial to set goals that are correct and achievable for the patient. As we often explain - why does a person need a perfectly developed hand? One needs it to write, another needs it to paint walls, etc. Each patient has different priorities. The goal of rehabilitation is to enable each patient to do what they need to do or to adapt them to a “new” life if they have a serious impairment. Rehabilitation is a process that involves an interdisciplinary team of specialists under the direction of a rehabilitation physician. Physical therapists work on motor function, speech and cognitive function with speech therapists, self-care functions with occupational therapists, and so on. And they all work together with the family, which is also part of this big team.

The goal of our approach is accomplished in 3 steps:
1. Getting to know the patient as a person, what is valuable to him, what is he like?
2. To form the patient's motivation for recovery and help him/her regain self-confidence and joy in life!
3. To return the patient to an active and fulfilling life based on their unique situation.
The key resource of our center is our close-knit team of professionals!
